A sociological phenomena

What is Migration

Migration is not only a physically explained displacement event, but also a sociologically significant change and self-renewal event. Even if there is a reason for this displacement, it is an indication that there is a defect that needs to be repaired or a gap that needs to be filled. Because if the conditions are as they should be, there is no search for a new home, no change is observed. In this case, it is clear that migration is a movement that seeks to achieve better and more efficient. In its simplest form, migration is an instinctive behavior.

What is the Migration of Tribes

It is the violent human migration to Europe between 350-800 years. It is divided into the first period and the second period. The first period of tribes migration includes intense border changes between the Western Roman Empire and the Huns. The migration of tribes in the second period is a continuation of the first period. The Hun group, which moved west around 350 BC get rid of Chinese domination in Central Asia , caused the Huns living between the Volga-Don rivers to migrate further west. At that time, the Goths, who were from the Germanic tribes, and the Proto-Slavs, the ancestors of the present-day Slavic peoples, lived in the plains to the north of the Black Sea. In 375, the Huns entered this region where the Goths and Proto-Slavs lived.

Ways to decrease migration

Reasons of Migration of Tribes

Apart from the pressure of the Chinese and Avars, the worsening of climatic conditions and drought is a situation that increases the desire and necessity of migration. The struggles between the tribes is also an event that pushes the people to migrate. As a result of the collapse of the Asian Hun State, the population concentrated around the Caspian and Aral Seas (West Asia), and the increase in the conflicts between the tribes in these areas and the westward shift of the population started the migration of tribes. These tribes migrated to Europe when the Huns, who came to the west of the Volga River, drove the tribes living there from their homeland. Tribes who migrated to Europe also affected Europe. These migrations proceeded with the referral and will of the Hun Khan Balamir at their head.

A Better and Fair World

Results of the Migration of Tribes

The Roman Empire was divided into two in 395. Western Roman Empire and Eastern Roman Empire (Byzantine) The Western Roman Empire, which could not stand the immigration, collapsed in 476. Europe remained in turmoil for nearly a century . The First Age ended and the Middle Ages began. Feudalism regime emerged in Europe. New nations emerged as a result of the merging of barbarian tribes with each other and with the Romans, and as a result, the contribution to the formation of the ethnic structure of today's European nations was very much. Christianity spread rapidly among barbarian tribes. The Germans left their mark on Medieval Europe by accepting Christianity. As a result, the church, papacy and scholastic thought began to gain power in the Middle Ages Europe. At the end of the migrations, Visigoths settled in today's Spain, Vandals in North Africa, Ostrogoths in Italy, and Franks in today's France.
